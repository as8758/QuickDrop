QuickDrop Privacy Policy

Last Updated: January 16, 2026

Overview

QuickDrop is a browser extension that helps you fill online forms faster by storing your information locally in your browser. We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring your data remains secure and private.

Data Collection

QuickDrop collects and stores only the information that you voluntarily provide through the setup form:

  • Personal identity information (first name, last name, legal name, email address, phone number)
  • Address information (street address, city, state, postal code, country)
  • Education history (schools, degrees, fields of study, GPA)
  • Work experience (job titles, companies, locations, dates, role descriptions)
  • Professional links (LinkedIn, GitHub, portfolio, resume URLs)
  • Any custom fields you choose to add

Data Storage

All data is stored locally on your device using Chrome's local storage API (chrome.storage.local). Your information:

  • Never leaves your browser
  • Is not transmitted to any external servers or cloud services
  • Is not shared, sold, or transferred to any third parties
  • Is not accessible to the extension developers
  • Remains under your complete control at all times

Data Usage

The information you provide is used exclusively for the following purposes:

  • Displaying your saved information in the extension's side panel interface
  • Populating form fields when you drag and drop data blocks
  • Inserting data when you click to paste into focused form fields
  • Providing right-click context menu options for quick field insertion

No other use of your data occurs. QuickDrop does not analyze, process, or utilize your data for any purpose beyond the core form-filling functionality.

Data Security

Since all data is stored locally within your browser's secure storage system, it is protected by:

  • Your browser's built-in security mechanisms
  • Your device's security settings and authentication
  • Chrome's extension sandboxing and isolation features

QuickDrop developers never have access to your data at any time, as it never leaves your device.

Third-Party Access

QuickDrop does not and will not:

  • Collect analytics, telemetry, or usage statistics
  • Use tracking cookies or any tracking mechanisms
  • Share, sell, or transfer data to third parties
  • Integrate with external services or APIs
  • Transmit any data over the internet
  • Include advertisements or third-party scripts

Data Retention and Deletion

Your data is retained locally in your browser until you choose to delete it. You can delete your data at any time by:

  • Uninstalling the QuickDrop extension (this removes all stored data)
  • Clearing your browser's extension data through Chrome settings
  • Editing or removing specific fields through the extension's setup page

Once deleted, your data cannot be recovered, as no backups are stored anywhere.

Permissions Explained

QuickDrop requests the following permissions to function properly:

  • storage: Required to save your form data locally in your browser's secure storage
  • activeTab: Allows the extension to insert data into form fields on the webpage you're currently viewing
  • contextMenus: Enables right-click menu options for quick data insertion
  • scripting: Necessary to inject content scripts that handle drag-and-drop functionality and form field population
  • sidePanel: Used to display the extension's interface in the browser's side panel
  • host permissions (<all_urls>): Allows the extension to work on any website where you need to fill forms

These permissions are used solely for the extension's core functionality and not for data collection or tracking.

Children's Privacy

QuickDrop does not knowingly collect or store information from children under the age of 13. The extension is designed for general use and does not target children. If you are under 13, please do not use this extension without parental supervision.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time to reflect changes in functionality or legal requirements. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last Updated" date. Continued use of the extension after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

Open Source

QuickDrop's source code is available for review, ensuring transparency in how the extension handles your data. You can verify that no data leaves your device by examining the code.

Contact Information

If you have any questions, concerns, or requests regarding this privacy policy or the QuickDrop extension, please contact us through the Chrome Web Store listing page or by opening an issue on our GitHub repository.

Your Rights

As all data is stored locally on your device, you have complete control over your information. You have the right to:

  • Access your data at any time through the extension interface
  • Modify or update your information through the setup page
  • Delete all or specific pieces of information
  • Export your data (by manually copying it from the extension)
  • Uninstall the extension and remove all associated data

Compliance

This privacy policy complies with:

  • Chrome Web Store Developer Program Policies
  • General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) principles
  • California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) requirements

Since no data is collected, processed, or transmitted by QuickDrop, traditional data protection obligations do not apply in the conventional sense. Your data privacy is protected by design.
